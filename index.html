Our Admission Process

Our Enrollment Process

We strongly believe in enrolling students based on performance and not on a first-come-first-served principle.
Our admission process is therefore ongoing throughout the entire year. Learners wishing to enter the Primary School are evaluated in an interview situation, and High School learners are required to write an entrance assessment in Mathematics and English. We do require prospective learners to be on our waiting list in order to write these assessments.

About our Academy

and our personalized learning model

We complement traditional instructive teaching with a personalised learning approach. Through this innovative learning concept and extensive after class support, we advance the intellectual, social, and personal development of our learners more effectively than conventional high schools. Our graduates are trained to be problem-solvers and to take calculated risks in an environment that is more than ever defined by uncertainty. Today's world is changing at a faster pace than ever before and good grades only no longer suffice. In addition to the CAPS curriculum, which is focused on cognitive development, practical and technical skills must also be developed by the time a learner graduates. It is for this reason that our approach prepares learners for "life after school" and emphasises personal development and study and career readiness.

Five reasons to choose us

And get a headstart

Our learners are pushed to set themselves apart

According to the Department of Education, 442 379 learners received a National Senior Certificate in 2016. Over 100 000 learners were progressed. In today's competitive environment, a secondary school education focusing only on cognitive development no longer suffices. This is why we coach our learners to develop an extensive set of character skills and practical life skills to set them apart from the crowd.

Unlimited career choices after graduation

Traditional schooling allows learners to disqualify themselves for many of the most interesting and sought-after university degree courses at an early age through their choice of elective subjects in Grade 10. At MMA, we ensure that all our graduates meet the requirements for a variety of degree courses so that they are not limited in their degree choice by subject decisions taken at the end of the Senior Phase.

Support of learners outside of class hours

We teach our learners in very small groups, which enables us to provide more comprehensive and more personalised guidance than traditional group sizes would permit. The average learner to educator ratio is 1:14. Extensive and regular extra classes is available outside of the regular classroom hours at the learner's, parent's, or educator's request and at no additional fee.

Education beyond the CAPS curriculum

Success in tertiary education and ultimately in labour markets now requires high levels of all-round competence. By the time our learners graduate from MMA, they have obtained a practical skillset in addition to the traditional secondary education which is based on CAPS at our Academy.

We appreciate diversity

Educators at M&M Academy foster a relationship with learners that we like to refer to as informally but respectful. We believe that learning new concepts – if done right – can be a highly stimulating experience. One of the many ways in which we pursue our mission to make our learners rediscover the joy in learning is to cultivate a climate where they can be themselves and are appreciated for their unique personal characteristics – a climate that appreciates diversity.

Christina Baumann

- Founder and CEO

Our learners graduate with more than a matric: They graduate fully prepared to take some of the most important decisions in their lives. Today's careers are often non-linear, but your choice of degree remains a crucial life decision that is often taken without sufficient experience of reflection.
Educators and staff at Montgomery & Miller Academy work together to prepare learners to take such decisions and – perhaps most importantly – to manage uncertainty and in their personal, academic, and eventually their professional lives. In addition to following the CAPS curriculum, we aim to equip our learners with additional practical skills and build their CVs at the same time. This way they develop – alongside social and interpersonal skills – the most sought-after competencies for tertiary education and the labour market.
We foster an inclusive, respectfully informal learning environment, in which no learner is left behind. Our team has an open door policy and offers extensive extracurricular guidance whenever required or desired by learners themselves. Individual time can be requested to review concepts encountered in class, or to provide guidance in non-academic matters, depending on the learner's needs. We believe that students must "take ownership" of their academic work to help them achieve intrinsic motivation. We support this intrinsic motivation by making coursework as engaging as possible, at all times highlighting practical relevance of the material, and its importance for university-level subjects and associated job opportunities. It is our job to find the best way to "make your child want to learn" the concepts needed for a successful start into tertiary education and professional life.

Christina Baumann

Deferred Payment Option

Would you like to change schools immediately but you have to give three months notice? No problem. We provide a solution. We understand that having to pay fees to two schools at the same time is almost impossible for most families. This is why we provide a deferred payment option for the parents of our learners. Pay a reduced fee for the first three months of enrollment and thereafter an increased fee for the remaining months of the year.
