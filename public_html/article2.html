The Best FREE Online Learning Resources

2 September Constantin Lisson Studying,

Regardless of the curriculum they follow, all students stand to benefit by branching out from the coursework and exposing themselves to other resources and methods of learning. In this instance, the internet is a powerful tool at both teachers’ and students’ disposal: There is an impressive variety of high-quality, stimulating and insightful learning tools, apps, websites and games available for free online. As with most things today, these resources can be found by browsing the web, but we’ve gone ahead and listed our favourites here:

Big History Project (BHP)

BHP is a project supported by the Gates Foundation and co-created by a talented team of teachers, students and tech experts. The project’s goal is to provide world class, classroom-ready resources to anyone, anywhere, for free. The courses available are designed with both teachers and the classroom in mind, providing more than 70 tried and tested lesson plans, high-quality videos, and texts to fit virtually any environment.

Codecademy

Codecademy is one of the most successful online learning resources dedicated specifically to teaching coding, and takes a revolutionary stance toward education. Where other coding sites follow an example/practice session workflow, Codecademy includes a live practice window. This means you can practice coding while still viewing the lesson material. The courses at Codecademy are well written and easy to follow and the website is organized very nicely. Codecademy features a centralized dashboard where you can monitor your progress, plus organizes lessons into complete modules. This lets you learn an entire language without needing to pick the next course manually. In their mission, they state that ‘education is broken’ and that they intend to ‘[build] the education the world needs’. They aim to help rebuild education, creating a more engaging, user-oriented educational experience.

PowerMyLearning

This NPO uses technology to strengthen the roles of teachers and parents in providing the necessary support required for students to excel in the classroom. PowerMyLearning has designed specific activities, guidelines and resources for students, teacher and parents. Students, for instance, are able to join various online classes, track their progress and compete with their friends, whereas parents are offered activities to connect with their child and monitor their progress.
